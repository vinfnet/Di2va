🔒 Why do I need to log in?

Di2va needs read-only access to your Strava activities to fetch ride data, GPS streams, cadence, and power — the inputs it uses to analyse your shifting. All processing happens entirely on your own computer. Your .FIT files are parsed locally, nothing is sent to any third-party service, and your Strava credentials never leave your machine.

For full details on how your data is handled, see the Setup Guide — Data Processing & Privacy.

How does this work? This analysis uses a deterministic, rules-based scoring engine — not a machine-learning model. It evaluates your shifting against established cycling best-practices (cadence range, cross-chain avoidance, gradient matching, shift smoothness) using data from the Strava API. We are investigating LLM-based approaches for richer, personalised feedback in a future release.
